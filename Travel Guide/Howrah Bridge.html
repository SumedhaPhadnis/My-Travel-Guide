HOWRAH BRIDGE

The Howrah Bridge is one of the most recognised landmarks in Kolkata. It conects Kolkata with its neighbouring industrial city of Howrah over the Hooghly river. The Howrah Bridge is constructed without using nuts and bolts, it is formed by riveting the entire steel structure . The brige was opened in 1943. Today, it is the sixth largest cantilever bridge in the world . On 14 June 1965 it was renamed Rabindra Setu after the great Bengali poet Rabindranath Tagore.
